# Part 1 - Designing a Mascot/Logo: My Journey With AI Image Generation

With the release of **Nanobana Pro**, I wanted to see how far I could push my little creative experiments. So far, it's been surprisingly great for generating the character I now use for my personal website and, of course, these posts.

This article is basically a _love letter_ to my mascot, **Undefined-chan**, and everything I've learned while trying to give her a consistent digital life.

<!-- Image Source: https://miro.medium.com/1*FnKKzMiDaieN59k0Re9JcQ.png | Local: /medium-backup-blog/medium-assets/13/img-01.png -->
![Accurate representation of my computer, bless Nanobana](/medium-backup-blog/medium-assets/13/img-01.png)

Tech often lacks cuteness, which is exactly why I lean into it. A character makes things prettier and way less boring, and, let's be honest, I'm too lazy to draw something that's basically complementary to the real work.

I wanted a mascot... I just don't want to spend five hours doing line art.

> I originally wanted to add a small disclaimer here, but that small disclaimer mutated into its own post: [About Prompts, AI and Playing with Nanobana Pro](https://medium.com/@mister.funable/about-prompts-ai-and-playing-with-nanobana-pro-3737479e6f53).

> **TL:DR:** when prompting AI, you still depend on luck. You can limit the possibilities, but you'll never have perfect control.

## The Beginning

Back when I was still debating whether starting a [YouTube channel](https://www.youtube.com/@MisterFunable) was worth it, I played with a couple ideas of how the whole "brand" should look.

The original plan? A **redhead retro pilot girl**, because that aesthetic is closer to mechas than anything else.

I also imagined a reddish color scheme, basically the total opposite of what I use in my videos now (green has completely taken over).

Getting the colors even halfway correct felt like a huge win. Asking for small adjustments, though, was pure trial and error, and "error" won most rounds.

Unfortunately, even when I liked this logo, it didn't look good at small sizes. Too many details vanished when scaled down. Which led me to the next iteration, something more minimalist.

### Second Iteration: Artlist

I changed the logo at around **40 subscribers**. Yes, popularity is hard. Please respect my struggle üòÇ

That was around the time when AI models started getting more consistent, so I experimented with a few options, including [Artlist](https://artlist.io/). I'm not 100% sure that's what I used back then (it was about a year ago), but I remember making several changes through it.
